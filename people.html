Sarah Slotznick

Assistant Professor of Earth Sciences

My research integrates field observations with laboratory measurements, incorporating diverse techniques such as rock magnetism, paleomagnetism, optical and electron microscopy, and x-ray spectroscopy.  Using these techniques, I probe questions spanning geologic time from the Modern to the Archean, with each time period providing distinct perspectives into the interaction of the biosphere and the Earth.

Email: sslotz [at] dartmouth [dot] edu

Curriculum Vitae

 

Postdoctoral Scholars

Joseph Biasi

NSF Postdoctoral Fellow

Joe combines fieldwork, igneous petrology, and paleo/aeromagnetism into his research.  His diverse research connects volcanic records to hazards, process, tectonic histories and paleoclimatic events.  His NSF Postdoctoral Fellowship is between Dartmouth and the University of Oregon to investigate eruptive timescales of the Columbia River Basalts.

Website

 

Graduate Students

Josephine Benson

MSc Student

Josie is co-advised by Wil Leavitt as her work pairs geochemistry and mineralogy.  She'll be exploring meromictic lakes in New Hampshire and Vermont to understand mineral formation processes using field geochemical observations, microscopy, and magnetic analyses.

 

Abdullah al Maruf

MSc Student

Abdullah joins the group from a physics, math, and material science background.  He will be exploring the magnetic properties of monoclinic and hexagonal pyrrhotite from a fundamental and observational standpoint.

Google Scholar

 

Olivia Moehl

MSc Student

Olivia is enthusiastic about field geology, mountains, and snow.  She spent a year as a Fulbright Scholar  at the Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU) learning about magnetism  and magnetic techniques across multiple scales.  While at Dartmouth, Olivia will dive into the Mesoproterozic Belt Supergroup of Montana with an aim at improving stratigraphic correlations of key fossil-bearing units.

 

 

Laurie Zielinski

PhD Student

Laurie has an academic curiosity about Earth History.  She will combine her background in geophysics fieldwork with a focus on Precambrian rocks to study red-beds throughout the Proterozoic.  Combining rock magnetism with paleomagnetism and microscopy, Laurie hopes to provide more robust constraints on how, when, and where hematite formed in these ancient samples.

 

 

Undergraduate Students

Jack Kreisler

Leave Term Grant S'21, Senior Thesis

Jack is fascinated by the intersection of geology, physics, and astronomy.  His work is focused on quantifying magnetite in modern marine environments to understand the controls that affect magnetite preservation and its potential as a proxy for oxygen and redox in ancient sedimentary rocks.

Samantha Lofman

Undergraduate Research Assistantship X'21, W'22, X'22

Sami is interested in current and paleo-climate change.  Her sophomore year she focused on doing field sampling and rock magnetic analyses on a feeder dike of the Columbia River Basalt before beginning work looking at modern lakes in New Hampshire.

Maria Grozeva

Leave Term Grant X'22, Presidential Scholar '22-'23

Mimi's project is focused on understanding connections between volcanic eruptions and climate change.  She performed fieldwork in Nova Scotia, Oregon, and Washington and is doing rock and paleomagnetic analyses throughout the academic year.

 

London Warburton

Senior Thesis

London is passionate about igneous rocks and glaciology. For their thesis, they chose to perform fieldwork and paleomagnetic analyses on basalts of the Central Atlantic Magmatic Province from Nova Scotia in order to better understand the eruptice timescales of this large igneous province.
